# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Aspiring bartenders, working hospitality professionals, hobbyists, and F&B operators who want to learn or advance in mixology, bartending, and broader food & beverage craft. They are hands-on people who value skill over theory — they come to the site looking for courses, credentials, and a reason to trust this academy over others.

## Product Purpose

Skill & Mix is a mixology, bartending, and F&B academy offering professional training courses. The site is the public face of the academy — informational, with a full landing page, course catalogue (list and detail), blog, and contact form. Success means a visitor understands what the academy offers, trusts its authority, and takes action (inquires, enrolls, or returns for content).

## Brand Personality

Freestyle, bold, creative. The brand feels like the best bartender in the room: technically skilled but never stiff, confident without being pretentious, visually expressive without being chaotic. It has energy.

## References

- **[des-todes.su](https://des-todes.su)** — Editorial design platform by Chueva Design. Borrow: typography as layout architecture (large bold display headlines placed asymmetrically as structural objects); the full-bleed horizontal image strip as a non-traditional hero; extreme scale contrast (very large vs. very small type, nothing in between); serif heading + uppercase monospace metadata below product cards; the arrow glyph (`→`) as a confident directional element; minimal nav with a low-key secondary CTA. Do not borrow: the light/cream background (Skill & Mix is dark), or the quiet tonal restraint — our palette has more heat.

## Anti-references

- Generic online course platforms (Udemy, Coursera aesthetics — grid of card thumbnails, sans-serif overload, soft blues)
- Corporate hospitality brands (stuffy hotel school vibes, muted palettes, stock photography of handshakes)
- Over-produced cocktail-app minimalism (all whitespace and tiny type, looks like a skincare brand)

## Design Principles

1. **Craft is visible** — like a well-made cocktail, every visual detail signals intention. No filler elements, no default choices.
2. **Bold personality, clean execution** — the brand is expressive in color, type, and layout; not in visual noise or decoration for its own sake.
3. **Expertise with energy** — the academy is serious about craft, but the site should feel alive. Authority and personality are not opposites.
4. **Show the work** — courses, instructors, and techniques lead. Let the product sell itself through specificity, not marketing language.
5. **Motion is choreography** — when elements move, they move like a practiced pour: purposeful, smooth, controlled.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Sufficient color contrast on all text. Reduced motion respected via `prefers-reduced-motion`. Keyboard navigable throughout.
